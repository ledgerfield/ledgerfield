Taxes

Step-by-step: run every NL tax calculator and extend to 100+ countries — all offline, no data leaves your device.

NL 2025 100+ jurisdictions Offline

Before you start

Open the Taxes tab (tab 3) in the app. All calculators on this page read from your entity configuration and your ledger balances — make sure tab 1 (Entity) and tab 2 (Ledger) are complete first.

Rates are loaded from rulesets that are distributed peer-to-peer. See the P2P sync guide to pull the latest published rulesets from the network before calculating.

VPB — Corporate Income Tax (Netherlands)

Applies to BV, Holding, and equivalent entities with NL jurisdiction. The calculator takes your taxable profit from the ledger and applies the current two-bracket rate.

Taxable profit Rate (2025)
Up to €200,000 19%
Above €200,000 25.8%
  1. On the Taxes tab, select VPB.
  2. Confirm the fiscal year and the pre-tax profit pulled from your Trial Balance.
  3. Apply any deductions (WBSO, investment allowances) — enter them in the deduction fields.
  4. The calculator outputs taxable profit, split across brackets, and total VPB due.
  5. Use Filing (tab 5) to export the VPB form as JSON or SAF-T XML.
The effective rate is shown separately so you can compare year-over-year without manually blending the brackets.

IB — Personal Income Tax (Netherlands)

Relevant for DGA salary, dividend distributions (Box 2), and savings/investments (Box 3). Select the IB calculator and choose the box.

1️⃣

Box 1 — Labour & home

36.97% up to the first-bracket threshold; 49.5% above. General tax credit (AHK) and employment credit (AK) are applied automatically to reduce the net amount due.

2️⃣

Box 2 — Substantial interest

24.5% on the first €67,000 of dividend / capital gain per person; 31% above that threshold.

3️⃣

Box 3 — Savings & investments

Forfait return of 6.04% applied to net assets above the tax-free threshold, then taxed at 36%. Enter total asset and debt values; the calculator derives the imputed return and final tax.

WBSO — R&D Tax Credit

WBSO reduces wage costs for approved R&D work. Enter your approved S&O hours and the applicable payroll base.

Standard rate

32% credit on the first band of S&O wage costs (up to €350,000).

Starter rate

40% credit on the first band for companies recognised as starters by RVO.

Above €350k

16% credit on S&O wage costs exceeding the first-band threshold.

Output

Total WBSO deduction, net employer cost after credit, and the figure to carry into the VPB deduction field.

DGA — Customary Salary Check

Dutch law requires a director-major-shareholder to draw a customary salary. The app compares the salary you have entered in the Payroll tab against the statutory benchmark.

  1. Go to the DGA check panel in the Taxes tab.
  2. The app reads gross annual salary from your payroll worker record.
  3. It compares your salary to the 2025 benchmark of €41,138.
  4. If your salary falls below the benchmark (and no exemption applies), a warn-box alert is shown with the shortfall amount.
  5. Correct the salary in tab 4 (Payroll) and recalculate.
Setting a DGA salary below €41,138 without a documented exemption is a recognised audit trigger. Correct it before filing.

BTW — VAT (Netherlands)

Standard rate is 21%, filed quarterly. The BTW calculator aggregates your ledger entries by VAT category for the period.

  1. Select the quarter and confirm the VAT number on file (Entity tab).
  2. The app groups ledger entries tagged BTW 21% into output VAT (sales) and input VAT (purchases).
  3. Net VAT due = output − input. The result is shown with a breakdown by category.
  4. Export the BTW declaration via tab 5 (Filing) as JSON or print to PDF.
  5. The deadline table in Filing lists the due date for each quarter.

CIT — Multi-country Corporate Tax

For entities outside the Netherlands (or holding structures spanning jurisdictions), LedgerField loads jurisdiction rulesets covering 100+ countries. Each ruleset is a CID-addressed JSON file — the CID guarantees the rates have not been tampered with.

  1. Go to the P2P tab (tab 7) and sync rulesets from the 5mart.ml relay — see the P2P sync guide.
  2. Return to Taxes and choose CIT — Other jurisdiction.
  3. Select the ISO jurisdiction. The matching ruleset is loaded and its CID is displayed for verification.
  4. Enter taxable profit and any locally deductible items. The calculator applies the bracket or flat rate from the ruleset.
  5. The effective rate is shown alongside the nominal rate for at-a-glance comparison.
You can also import a ruleset manually: download the JSON from a peer, paste it into the P2P tab import field, and the app recomputes the CID to verify authenticity before loading it.
