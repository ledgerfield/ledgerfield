Bookkeeping

The Ledger tab is where all double-entry bookkeeping happens. Every transaction is a journal entry against your chart of accounts. Nothing leaves your device — the vault stays offline.

1. Post a journal entry

  1. Open the app and go to tab 2 Ledger. Open LedgerField
  2. Click New entry. Fill in the date and an optional description.
  3. Add at least two lines — one debit, one credit — so that totals balance:
    FieldWhat to enter
    AccountPick from your chart of accounts (NL RGS / UK FRS102 / US GAAP).
    SideDebit or Credit.
    AmountPositive number in your entity's currency.
    CategoryOptional tag for filtering (e.g. sales, travel).
  4. Click Save. The entry appears in the ledger list immediately.
Debits and credits must balance before you can save. The app shows the running difference in real time.

2. Import transactions from CSV

Bank exports and spreadsheets can be imported directly. Use a semicolon-delimited file with this exact column order:

date;description;amount;category
2025-01-15;Office supplies;-84.70;expenses
2025-01-17;Invoice #1042 — client payment;3500.00;sales
2025-01-31;Monthly SaaS subscription;-49.00;software
  1. In the Ledger tab, click Import CSV.
  2. Select your file. The app previews the parsed rows before committing.
  3. Choose which account each row maps to for the debit and credit sides, then click Confirm import.
Amounts are signed: negative = money out (credit the bank), positive = money in (debit the bank). The importer enforces this convention automatically.

3. Run the Trial Balance

The trial balance checks that total debits equal total credits across all accounts for any date range.

  1. In the Ledger tab, click Trial Balance.
  2. Set the From and To dates (defaults to your financial year).
  3. The table lists every account with its debit total, credit total, and net balance. The footer row shows the grand totals — they must match.
A zero difference in the footer means your books are in balance. If it is non-zero, the imbalance row is highlighted so you can find the offending entry quickly.

4. Run the Profit & Loss

The Profit & Loss report groups income and expense accounts to show your net result for the period.

  1. Click Profit & Loss in the Ledger tab.
  2. Choose the period. The report segments into Revenue, Cost of Sales, Gross Profit, Operating Expenses, and Net Profit.
  3. Export to JSON or SAF-T XML from the Filing tab when you are ready to file.
📂

Chart of accounts

NL RGS, UK FRS102, and US GAAP are built in. The chart is editable: add sub-accounts under any standard code without breaking existing entries.

🔒

Fully offline

Every entry is encrypted in the browser vault. Raw books are never transmitted — only anonymised aggregates and public tax rulesets ever touch the network.

📤

SAF-T XML export

One click from the Filing tab produces a standards-compliant SAF-T XML that most tax authorities and auditors accept directly.
