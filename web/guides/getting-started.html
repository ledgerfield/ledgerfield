Getting started

LedgerField runs entirely in your browser — no install, no account, no server. Follow these five steps and you will have a working ledger in under two minutes.

  1. Open the app. Navigate to ledgerfield/app.html. The page is a single self-contained HTML file. Once loaded it works fully offline; you can even save it to disk and open it from there.
  2. Set a vault password. On first launch the Vault tab (tab 6) opens automatically. Choose a password of at least 8 characters. All data is encrypted with PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 before being written to local storage. You can export an encrypted backup at any time from the same tab.
  3. Add your first entity. Go to tab 1 — Entity — and fill in:
    FieldNotes
    NameLegal name of the company or person
    TypeBV, Holding, ZZP, VOF, Ltd, LLC, Corp, GmbH, or SARL
    ISO jurisdictionTwo-letter country code, e.g. NL, GB, US
    Registration numberKvK, EIN, UTR — whichever applies
    VAT numberOptional; required for BTW filing
    Financial-year startDay and month, e.g. 01-01
    Parent entityOptional; use for holding structures
  4. Pick a chart of accounts. Still on tab 1, select the standard that matches your jurisdiction:
    🇳🇱

    NL RGS

    Dutch Referentie Grootboekschema — default for BV and ZZP entities with jurisdiction NL.

    🇬🇧

    UK FRS 102

    UK Financial Reporting Standard for small and micro entities.

    🇺🇸

    US GAAP

    Standard chart for US-based LLC, Corp, or other entity types.

  5. Post your first entries. Switch to tab 2 — Ledger — and start adding journal entries: pick debit and credit accounts, enter the amount and category. You can also import a CSV file (format: date;description;amount;category). See the bookkeeping guide for a full walkthrough of the Ledger tab, Trial Balance, and Profit & Loss report.
Everything stays on your device. Raw books, entity details, and vault keys never leave the browser. Only public artefacts — anonymised aggregates and tax rulesets — can be shared over the network, and only when you explicitly choose to do so via the P2P or Data market tabs. See the offline-first privacy model for details.
