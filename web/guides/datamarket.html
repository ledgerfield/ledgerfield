Data market

The LedgerField data market lets your accounting data generate revenue without exposing it. You contribute to pooled statistics; buyers receive only anonymised summaries. Raw books never leave your device — ever.

Privacy guarantee: only two artefact types ever traverse the knitweb network — public tax rulesets and anonymised statistical aggregates. Individual entries, entity details, payroll records, and vault keys remain offline at all times.

How it works

  1. Grant consent per data category and jurisdiction.
  2. Contribute: LedgerField reads the relevant figures from your local ledger and holds them locally.
  3. Wait for the k-anonymity threshold — at least 5 distinct nodes must contribute before any aggregate is publishable.
  4. Publish: a data package is generated, addressed by its CID, and listed in the market.
  5. Earn: buyers browse and purchase packages; proceeds are split automatically between the platform and contributors.

Step 1 — Grant consent

Open the Datamarkt tab (tab 8). You will see a grid of the 7 data categories across every jurisdiction you have configured. Toggle each cell independently — for example, share payroll data for NL but withhold it for DE.

7

Data categories

The app lists all 7 categories in the consent grid. Each is labelled in the UI. You choose which ones to enable, per jurisdiction, before any data leaves the device.

🌍

Per-jurisdiction control

Consent is scoped to the combination of category + ISO jurisdiction code. Withdrawing consent removes your node from future aggregations for that cell immediately.

Consent can be updated or revoked at any time. Changes take effect on the next aggregation cycle — they do not retroactively alter already-published packages.

Step 2 — Contribute values

After granting consent, LedgerField reads the relevant figures from your local ledger for each enabled category and jurisdiction. The values are prepared locally — nothing is transmitted at this stage. Your node registers participation with the public relay at 5mart.ml using only its anonymous node ID (a SHA-256 hash containing no personal information).

You can inspect which figures will be contributed from the Datamarkt tab before any publish action is taken.

Step 3 — The k ≥ 5 threshold

Publication is refused below k = 5. An aggregate can only be published when at least 5 distinct nodes have contributed to that category + jurisdiction combination. If fewer than 5 peers have enrolled, the Publish button is disabled and no market listing is created. This hard threshold prevents statistical re-identification of any individual contributor.

The node count is verified at publish time against the relay's contributor log. Each node is identified solely by its anonymous node ID — no IP address, company name, or personal data is recorded.

Participating nodes Can publish? Reason
1 – 4 No Below k-anonymity threshold; publish button disabled
5 or more Yes Threshold met; aggregate is publishable

Step 4 — Publish a data package

Once the threshold is met, click Publish in the Datamarkt tab. LedgerField performs four steps automatically:

  1. Computes the statistical aggregate from all contributing nodes for the selected category + jurisdiction.
  2. Serialises the result as a structured JSON data package (category, jurisdiction, statistics, node count, timestamp).
  3. Computes the CID of the package — a content-addressed fingerprint that is unique to the exact bytes published. Any tampering changes the CID.
  4. Broadcasts the package to the market via the 5mart.ml relay. The CID becomes the package's permanent, verifiable address.
The CID is displayed after publishing. Share it with buyers who want to verify the package independently by recomputing it from the downloaded file.

Step 5 — Browse and buy

The market browser lists all published data packages with their category, jurisdiction, contributing node count, price, and CID. Click a package to preview its metadata and summary statistics, then purchase to download the full aggregate.

Buyers can verify authenticity by recomputing the CID from the downloaded JSON and comparing it to the listed value — any mismatch indicates tampering.

Purchases are settled on-device. The app records your acquisition history and lets you re-download previously purchased packages by CID.

Earnings and the leaderboard

Platform fee — 10%

Ten percent of each sale is retained by the platform to fund relay infrastructure and market operation. This fee is fixed and applied automatically at settlement.

Contributor share — 90%

The remaining ninety percent is distributed to all nodes that contributed to that package. Distribution is equal by default; an optional weighted mode allocates proportionally to contribution size or data quality score.

Leaderboard

The Datamarkt tab shows a live leaderboard of top-earning nodes, ranked by cumulative earnings across categories. Nodes appear by their anonymous node ID only — no company name or personal data is exposed. Use the leaderboard to track your own ranking and identify which categories attract the most buyer demand.

What leaves your device — summary

Only these two artefact types ever traverse the network:
  • Public tax rulesets (not personal; any node can publish or import them)
  • Anonymised statistical aggregates — and only once k ≥ 5 distinct nodes have contributed
Journal entries, entity details, payroll records, vault keys, and any other raw data never leave your device.
