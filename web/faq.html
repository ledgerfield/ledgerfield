Frequently asked questions

Short answers, with links into the wiki and guides for depth.

Is my financial data safe?
Yes. Your books live in an encrypted vault (PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256) inside your own browser. LedgerField is offline-first — raw data never leaves your device.
Do I need an internet connection?
No. The whole app is a single HTML file that runs offline. A connection is only needed if you choose to sync public tax rulesets or publish anonymised aggregates — see the P2P sync guide.
Which countries are supported?
Chart of accounts and the tax engine cover 100+ jurisdictions, with 25 CID-addressed 2025 rulesets shipping today. See the full coverage matrix.
How do tax rates stay up to date?
Rates live in rulesets addressed by CID and shared peer-to-peer via gossip. You verify any ruleset by recomputing its content ID — no trust in a server required.
What is the data market, and is it private?
An opt-in marketplace for anonymised benchmarks. You consent per category, and an aggregate is only publishable at k≥5 distinct contributors. Only summaries ever leave your device — see the data-market guide.
Is it free and open source?
Yes — the project is open source on GitHub.
Can I manage multiple entities?
Yes. Add any number of entities (BV, Holding, ZZP, Ltd, GmbH…) with parent/holding structures; reports and filings are scoped per entity.
How do I back up my data?
Export an encrypted backup of your vault from the Vault tab, then restore it on any device with your password.
Does it replace my accountant?
No — it is a tool. You keep the books and compute figures locally, then export JSON or SAF-T XML to hand to your accountant or file with the authority.
What is the knitweb, and who runs the relay?
The knitweb is the peer-to-peer layer (built on pulse) that carries public artefacts. The default public relay is 5mart.ml, but the mesh works peer-to-peer and you can run your own.
